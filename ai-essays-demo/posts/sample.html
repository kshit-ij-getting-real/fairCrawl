When AIs become the main readers of the web (demo)

Demo post · AI Essays
Demo post used for FairFetch testing. This is not a real publication or product announcement.

For most of the web’s life, the unit of attention was a human pageview. You loaded a page, you saw the layout, you maybe saw an ad. The business model sat on top of your eyeballs.

As large language models and agents spread, more and more “readers” of the web are going to be machines. They don’t see ads. They don’t click subscribe buttons. They just fetch, parse, and compress.

If the main consumers of online writing are AIs, then the current way of paying for that writing breaks. Either everything moves behind private APIs and walled gardens, or we build a new layer where machines can ask for content under clear rules and pay tiny amounts when they do.

This page is a small, stable target for that world: a simple article with clear structure that an AI agent can read through a marketplace instead of scraping blindly.
