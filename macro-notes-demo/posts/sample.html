Washington is falling (demo excerpt)

Demo post · Macro Notes
Demo article used to test FairFetch. This is not a real investment or political recommendation.

The centre of gravity for power used to be obvious. It sat in capital cities, on trading floors, and around a handful of newsrooms that decided what counted as “the story”.

Now it lives in feeds, group chats, and opaque models that remix the last few decades of online text into instant answers. The timelines of markets, politics, and culture have collapsed into the same screen.

When institutions move on quarterly or electoral cycles, but capital and information move at machine speed, something has to give. The old system is still pretending it can keep up by issuing statements and holding hearings. The new system is already routing around it.

This page exists as a simple, stable target for AI agents. It shows how long-form writing can stay on the open web, while still being read under clear rules and, eventually, paid for.

In a real publication, this post would link out to data, sources, and follow-up essays. For now, it is just a small, predictable anchor on the shifting web.
